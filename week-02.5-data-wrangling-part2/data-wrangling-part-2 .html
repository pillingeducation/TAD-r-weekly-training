select()

It’s not uncommon to get datasets with hundreds or even thousands of variables. In this case, the first challenge is often narrowing in on the variables you’re actually interested in.

select() allows you to rapidly zoom in on a useful subset using operations based on the names of the variables.

mutate()

If you want to add new columns that are functions of existing columns. That’s the job of mutate().

mutate() always adds new columns at the end of your dataset so we’ll start by creating a narrower dataset so we can see the new variables.

mutate() can be used with operations (*, +, etc) and is often used with if_else() or case_when()
